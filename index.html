I. GETTING STARTED: YOUR FIRST RAFT

To create a Raft, go to the CREATE tab. You will need:

  • A Secret Payload: The data you want to protect (keys, seeds, etc.).
  • A Master Password: A 12-word phrase that only you know. This is your primary key.
  • A Raft Name: A public label so your heirs can identify it later.

Once you encrypt your data, the system generates three Recovery Phrases (A, B, and C). You only need any TWO of these to get your data back.

II. THE PULSE: STAYING IN CONTROL

As long as you are active, your data remains locked. You prove you are active by transmitting a Pulse from the OWNER DASHBOARD.

If you fail to transmit a Pulse within your chosen timeframe (e.g., 180 days), the system assumes you are gone and prepares to release the first Recovery Phrase to your heirs.

III. THE 2-OF-3 RULE: YOUR SAFETY NET

The Raft splits your security into three "fragments" to ensure no single person can access your data without your permission:

  1. Network Fragment: Stored securely on the Nostr network at your Heir Recovery Identity. It is released only if your Pulse sequence stops.
  2. Heir Fragment 1: A physical share you give to your first heir.
  3. Heir Fragment 2: A second physical share for another heir or a backup location.

Recovery requires any two fragments. Your heirs use the Vault ID printed on their cards to find the Network Fragment and combine it with their physical Recovery Fragment to unlock the vault.

IV. SECURITY: WHY IT'S SAFE

We use industry-standard AES-256 encryption. Your data is encrypted before it ever leaves your computer. We never see your password, and your secrets are never stored in "the cloud" in a readable format.

Private by Design: Your Master Password never leaves your machine. When you transmit a "Pulse," the application uses your password locally to sign a cryptographic heartbeat using the Nostr protocol. Relays on the network can verify that you sent the message because of your unique digital signature, but they never see your actual password. This ensures your safety even on a compromised network.

By using Nostr, your encrypted Raft is spread across a global "swarm" of relays, making it nearly impossible to delete or censor.

V. SOVEREIGN & EXTINCTION-PROOF

The Raft is designed to outlive companies, websites, and even the current internet infrastructure.

  • Offline Use: You can download this entire app as a single file and run it on a computer that has no internet.
  • The Blueprint: Every Raft includes an "AI Reconstruction Prompt"—a set of instructions that allows an AI to rebuild the decryption logic from scratch if this website ever disappears.

The "Shirt On Your Back" Scenario: In a worst-case disaster where you lose all hardware and the raft-protocol website goes offline, you and your heirs only need your physical phrase fragments and the "Blueprint" below. Using any basic computer and a modern AI, you can reconstruct the decryption tool locally and recover your payload from the Nostr network.

🏗️ Why Nostr? (The Sovereign Infrastructure)

Standard cloud infrastructure (AWS, Google Docs, iCloud) is built on the concept of permission. Your data exists at the pleasure of a corporation that can lock your account, change its terms, or vanish entirely. Raft uses Nostr because it is a decentralized, censorship-resistant relay network where you own your identity via mathematics, not a login. There are no "accounts" to ban and no central servers to seize. By avoiding traditional infrastructure, Raft ensures your legacy is protected by the swarm, not a terms-of-service agreement.

🔄 Functional Flows

For the first time in human history, a majority of our assets are digital or protected by digital passwords and keys. With increased requirements for multi-factor authentication or reliance on external companies it is dangerously easy to lock yourself or your heirs from your assets. Raft is a solution to this new problem.

1. The Creation Flow (Sovereign Setup)

This flow establishes the secure Raft and its decentralized backup.

  • Flexible Master Password: Users can choose any 12-word Master Password. The Raft owner's master password must be memorable to the owner. Raft's only strict rule is that the password consists of 12 words. From a cryptographic perspective, 12 random BIP-39 words are ideal, and the app has a button for that. From a human memory point of view, other selections may be better. Raft lets the user find their ideal balance. This can range from a high-entropy, "impossible to crack" BTC-standard mnemonic to an easy-to-remember set of words, allowing users to balance memorability vs. security according to their needs.

  • Entropy Generation: The generateRandomPass tool is available for users who prefer a cryptographically secure, 128-bit entropy mnemonic.

  • Encryption: runEncrypt uses the Master Password to protect data.

  • Twin Rafts: runPublish broadcasts identical copies of the encrypted Raft to both the Owner's DDK and the Heir's DDK (derived from the Vault ID). This ensures maximum availability.

  • High-Security 2-of-3 Split: The Raft Key is split into 3 backup shares. While the Master Password can be user-defined for memorability, the Heir Keys (Phrase B and C) are always generated by Raft using the highest security standards (SSS + BIP-39 mapping).

    • Phrase A (Network): Sent to the Nostr swarm, locked by a Dead Man's Switch (DMS).
    • Phrase B (Heir 1): Distributed physically to the first guardian.
    • Phrase C (Heir 2): Distributed physically to the second guardian.

  • Physical Backup (Heir Records): The system generates a high-quality 4-page print layout (Owner, Heir A, Heir B, Heir C). Each page includes unique Batch IDs and clear instructions for the respective holder. This ensures a physical "last resort" backup that is cryptographically linked to the specific phrase generation event.

  • Independent Heirs (Advanced): Users can reuse their Master Passphrase to create multiple Rafts. Because each Raft uses a unique cryptographic salt, each will generate a completely independent set of phrases (A, B, C). This prevents different heir groups (e.g., family vs. lawyers) from colluding.

  • Self-Test: verifySelfTest ensures that any combination of 2 phrases can successfully recreate the key before deploying.

2. The Maintenance Flow (Proof-of-Life)

This flow prevents the inheritance mechanism from triggering prematurely.

  • Heartbeat: sendPulse sends a Kind 9000 heartbeat to the Nostr swarm. SendPulse requires the owner to find his Raft and enter his master key, thus building memory required for "shirt on my back only" recovery.

  • DMS Lock: As long as heartbeats are received within the "expiry" window, Phrase A remains hidden from the network.

  • Evolution: updateRaftContent allows updating a Raft's payload in-place directly from the dashboard by reusing the session's Raft Key. Note: To change a Raft's name or owner, a new Raft must be created and the old one deleted.

3. The "Shirt on my back only" Recovery Flow (Owner)

The owner can recover their Raft from memory alone without needing heirs or physical backups.

  • Anonymous Identity (DDK): Raft unlinks Nostr IDs from real people. Your public Nostr identity (Deterministic Discovery Key) is generated purely from your chosen Owner Name and the first 3 words of your Master Password, ensuring privacy.

  • Swarm Discovery: discoverRafts scans the swarm for your encrypted Kind 30023 events using your DDK.

  • Direct Decryption: runDecrypt (Master Pass Mode) uses the full 12-word Master Password to decrypt the Raft. (The 3-word subset is for discovery/pulsing; the full 12-word set is required for decryption and deletion).

  • Raft Owner Responsibility: Remember their 12-word master password, Owner Name and how to find the Raft app. That is all, and it is practiced with each heartbeat send.

4. The Inheritance Flow (Heirs)

Allows heirs to recover the Raft if the owner is missing or deceased.

  • Oracle Check: If the DMS expires (no pulses detected), the Nostr swarm releases Phrase A to designated heirs.

  • Proof of Custody: sendHeirPulse allows heirs to broadcast a Kind 9001 pulse, signaling to the owner that their backup shares are still active and accessible. The Owner Dashboard tracks the latest date any heir has pulsed for a specific Raft, providing a single "High Water Mark" of guardian readiness.

  • 2-of-3 Reconstruction: runDecrypt (SSS Mode) combines the network's Phrase A with either Phrase B or Phrase C (held by the heirs) to decrypt the Raft.

  • Manual Bypass: Heirs can also meet physically to combine Phrase B + Phrase C, bypassing the network entirely.

5. The Extinction-Proof Flow (Local Resilience)

  • Export: saveOfflineApp packages the entire app into a single HTML file.

  • Protocol Blueprint: The "Emergency Reconstruction Prompt" provides the exact logic for rebuilding the protocol using standard cryptographic libraries (OpenPGP.js, secrets.js, PBKDF2).

  • AI Emergency Reconstruction Logic: The primary flows of Raft (encrypt, retrieve, and decrypt) are described as AI prompts. This is useful if, in the future, the web app needs to be replaced.

6. The Air-Gapped Flow (Maximum Security)

For users requiring the highest level of security, Raft supports completely offline operation.

  • Offline Encryption: Users can run a local copy of Raft on an air-gapped machine to generate their Raft and phrases.

  • Payload Export: exportOfflinePayload generates a secure JSON file containing the encrypted Raft and metadata.

  • Safe Transmission: This payload can be moved via physical media to an online terminal and uploaded using importOfflinePayload for broadcast to the Nostr swarm.

  • Zero-Touch Keys: This ensures that the Master Password and the cleartext Raft never exist on a machine with network access.

⛵ The Minimal Raft Co. (The Moat)

(Note: Raft Co. is currently only a proposal/idea and does not yet exist as a formalized entity. The following represents the intended future service model.)

In a world of AI-generated software, the value of a tool must exist beyond its code and IP. Raft Co. would function as a Resilience Utility—it would not own your data; it would ensure your data's survival.

1. Data Persistence SLA (The Assurance Relay)

While public Nostr relays are "best-effort," Raft Co. would provide a specialized relay cluster that whitelists and persists inheritance events (Kind 30023, 9000, 30078). Users would pay for a Sovereign Service Level Agreement (SLA) to guarantee their "Phrase A" is available when their heirs need it most.

2. The Protocol Help Desk (Confidence as a Service)

The most critical part of inheritance isn't the key—it's the process. Raft Co. would provide human-led, non-custodial guidance for heirs during states of grief or stress, helping them navigate the recovery protocol without ever touching their private keys.

3. Physical Survival Kits

Raft Co. would bridge the digital-physical gap by selling "Survival Kits": titanium seed plates for Phrases B and C, and durable, high-contrast "Instruction for Heirs" cards that survive floods, fires, and time.

4. The "Raft Health" Dashboard

A real-time monitoring service that would audit the user's "Resilience Score" across both private and public relay swarms, providing proactive alerts if data redundancy falls below safe levels.

The Extinction-Proof Mandate: If Raft Co. were to vanish tomorrow, the protocol remains unbroken. The company would be a convenience and reliability booster, not a dependency. The "Moat" is the brand's commitment to resilience and the physical infrastructure that supports it.
